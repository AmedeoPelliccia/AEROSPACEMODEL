## Applicable Regulations

### EASA Part-M — Continuing Airworthiness
- **Subpart B (Continuing Airworthiness Management):** Storage and preservation activities are part of the continuing airworthiness plan; minimum inspection intervals defined in CAMO.
- **Evidence required:** Storage inspection records, CAMO-approved storage programme.

### EASA Part-145 — Maintenance Organisation Approvals
- **145.A.25 (Facility requirements):** Hangars and parking bays used for maintenance must be of adequate size, protection from weather, and have appropriate lighting.
- **Evidence required:** Facility approval documentation, hangar capacity certification.

### FAA AC 43.13-1B — Acceptable Methods, Techniques, and Practices
- **Chapter 8 (Aircraft Inspection and Maintenance):** Tie-down and mooring procedures accepted by FAA.
- **Evidence required:** Compliance with aircraft-specific mooring diagram; inspection records.

### ICAO Annex 14 — Aerodromes
- **Volume I, Chapter 9 (Visual Aids):** Parking bay markings and lighting requirements.
- **Evidence required:** Aerodrome design compliance documentation.

## Applicable Standards

### NFPA 2 — Hydrogen Technologies Code (H₂ Parking Bay) ⭐
- **Section 7 (Indoor Storage and Use):** Parking bays where LH₂-fuelled aircraft are parked with fuel aboard require H₂ hazardous area classification, forced ventilation, H₂ detection, and bonding/grounding.
- **Evidence required:** Bay ventilation design, H₂ detector calibration records, electrical ATEX certification.

### ISO 45001 — Occupational Health and Safety
- **Clause 8.1 (Operational planning and control):** Safe work procedures for parking, mooring, and towing operations.
- **Evidence required:** Safe system of work documentation, toolbox talk records.

### EASA Special Condition SC-28-H2-001 ⭐
- **Scope:** LH₂ boil-off during parking must be managed; vent stacks or boil-off recovery connections are required in enclosed bays.
- **Evidence required:** Boil-off management design documentation, vent stack capacity calculation.
