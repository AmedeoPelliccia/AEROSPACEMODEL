# ATA 10I — Parking, Mooring, Storage, and RTS Infrastructure: Procedures

**Domain:** I-INFRASTRUCTURES / M2-MAINTENANCE_ENVIRONMENTS / ATA_10  
**Document Type:** Operational Procedures  
**Revision:** 0.1.0

---

## 1. Purpose

This document defines step-by-step procedures for parking, mooring, storage, and return-to-service activities at M2 maintenance environment facilities.

---

## 2. Standard Parking Procedure

### 2.1 Pre-Parking Checklist

- [ ] Confirm parking position available and suitable for aircraft type
- [ ] Ground equipment cleared from position
- [ ] Marshaller and ground crew briefed
- [ ] GPU/services availability confirmed
- [ ] For H₂ aircraft: ⭐ Confirm H₂-rated position; H₂ monitors active

### 2.2 Parking Execution

| Step | Responsible | Action |
|------|-------------|--------|
| 1 | Marshaller | Guide aircraft to position on centerline |
| 2 | Marshaller | Give STOP signal at designated stop bar |
| 3 | Ground crew | Apply nose gear chocks (forward and aft) |
| 4 | Ground crew | Apply main gear chocks (forward and aft, both sides) |
| 5 | Ground crew | Attach bonding cable to aircraft ground point |
| 6 | Ground crew | Connect ground power (if required) |
| 7 | Ground crew | Install any required safety devices (ground locks, etc.) |
| 8 | Supervisor | Confirm aircraft secured; sign parking record |

---

## 3. Mooring Procedure

### 3.1 Standard Mooring (Wind ≤ 40 kt)

Follow AMM ATA 10 mooring diagram. Typical sequence:
1. Attach nose tie-down line to nose gear and apron anchor point.
2. Attach wing tip tie-downs (port and starboard).
3. Attach tail tie-down.
4. Verify all lines taut but not overtensioned.

### 3.2 Storm Mooring (Wind > 40 kt forecast)

Additional tie-downs applied per AMM extended mooring diagram. Facilities must have adequate apron anchor points installed (see 02_DESIGN_SPEC.md Section 3).

---

## 4. Storage Preparation Procedure

### 4.1 Entering Short-Term Storage

1. Park aircraft per Section 2 above.
2. Apply AMM ATA 10 storage configuration.
3. Complete storage entry log (date, storage type, responsible person).
4. Schedule first inspection (7 days or per operator program).

### 4.2 Entering Long-Term Storage

1. Complete short-term storage steps.
2. ⭐ Defuel LH₂ system per ATA 28 defueling procedure (REQ-10I-046).
3. Apply AMM extended storage program (engine preservation, etc.).
4. Notify airworthiness department to activate storage maintenance program.

---

## 5. Return to Service (RTS) Procedure

### 5.1 Post-Storage RTS Checklist

- [ ] Remove all wheel chocks, safety pins, covers
- [ ] Aircraft external general visual inspection (GVI) — no visible damage
- [ ] Tire pressure check (per AMM ATA 32)
- [ ] Fluid level checks (hydraulic, oil, water)
- [ ] ⭐ LH₂ tank pressure and temperature check (per AMM ATA 28)
- [ ] ⭐ H₂ system integrity check (no leak alarms)
- [ ] Battery charge level check
- [ ] Review of any pending deferred defects
- [ ] Release to service sign-off by Part-145 approved person

### 5.2 RTS After Specific Events

| Event | Additional RTS Actions |
|-------|----------------------|
| Heavy gust or storm | Structural inspection per AMM |
| Bird strike during parking | Damage assessment |
| Lightning strike | EWIS inspection per AMM |
| ⭐ H₂ leak alert during storage | ⭐ H₂ system full inspection per ATA 28 before release |

---

## 6. Emergency Release Procedure

If aircraft must be moved urgently (fire, emergency):

1. Remove all chocks (minimum: at least nose chocks for towing direction).
2. Confirm tow bar connected and tow vehicle attached.
3. Notify ATC/coordinator of emergency movement.
4. ⭐ If H₂ aircraft: Confirm H₂ safety status before movement (if time allows); evacuate non-essential personnel from H₂ zone.
5. Move aircraft to safe area.
6. Apply emergency chocks in new position.
