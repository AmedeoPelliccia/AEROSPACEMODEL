## AEROSPACEMODEL

The overarching Lifecycle Operating System (LOS) that governs all programme execution, product data management, and publication workflows across IDEALEeu Enterprise. AEROSPACEMODEL provides the canonical folder structure, governance policies, automation infrastructure, and lifecycle phases (LC00–LC14) for all products and programmes. It is the "operating system" within which individual programmes (AMPEL360 Q100, GAIA-SPACE-LAUNCHER, SPACET Q10) execute.

## AMPEL360 Q100 (AMPEL360-AIR-T)

The flagship product programme: a ~100-passenger regional aircraft concept featuring a Blended Wing Body airframe, H₂ PEM fuel cell primary power, distributed/open-fan propulsors, peak-power buffering, and Digital Product Passport foundations. The "Q100" designates the passenger class; "AMPEL360" is the programme identifier. The dual-path configuration supports both BWB and WTW airframe variants.

## AQUA-V

A quality and validation framework providing structured validation workflows and quality metrics across lifecycle phases within the AEROSPACEMODEL ecosystem.

## ASIGT (Artificial Super Intelligence Generative Transformer)

The governance-first layer of the dual ASIT/ASIGT repository architecture. The name declares the architectural ambition: a generative-transformer paradigm operating at the ASI (Artificial Super Intelligence) horizon, where the governance skeleton — policies, roles, workflows, validation rules — is defined before any content generation occurs. ASIGT establishes the rules; ASIT generates within them. Governance precedes content.

## ASIT (A-S-I Type)

The content-generation layer of the dual ASIT/ASIGT architecture. "A-S-I Type" classifies the artifact by its position on the intelligence spectrum (Artificial → Super → Intelligence), establishing the type-level identity for generated content. ASIT contains actual engineering and publication content, constrained by the governance rules established in → ASIGT. Content follows governance.

## Body+Brain Identity Register

A registry of 312 intelligent artifacts across the AMPEL360 programme, classifying each as a physical "body" component, a computational "brain" component, or a hybrid of both. This register supports the → PR-O-RO PROTOROBOTICS framework.

## C² CELL (Circular Cryogenic Cell)

The reframing of the traditional fuel system domain (ATA 28) for hydrogen-powered aircraft. "C² CELL" denotes the hydrogen storage, distribution, and management infrastructure — emphasising circularity (lifecycle material recovery) and cryogenic conditions (LH₂ at ~20 K). Replaces the inherited "Fuel" label to reflect the fundamentally different physics and safety ontology of hydrogen as → Prime Material.

## CAOS (Computer Aided Operations & Services)

The fourth pillar of digital engineering, extending the classical CAD/CAE/CAM triad. CAOS encompasses continuous airworthiness, operational sustainment, in-service data feedback, reliability programmes, and technical services — digitally managed as a first-class engineering discipline rather than a downstream afterthought.

## CAXS (Continuous Aircraft Cross-Sustainment — The Exchange Portal, CA360)

The exchange portal for continuous aircraft cross-sustainment operations. CAXS provides structured lifecycle entry points for sustainment activities, enabling cross-programme and cross-fleet data exchange. Operates as the CA360 portal interface, linking CAOS operations to specific lifecycle phases. Example: the LC04 scaffold for SPACET Q10 with TALF integration.

## CB→QB→UE→FE→FWD→QS (Bridge Layer Chain)

The six quantum-classical bridge layers of the → TFA: Classical Bridge → Quantum Bridge → Unified Entanglement → Functional Entanglement → Forward Projection → Quantum State. This chain formalises the transition pathway from classical engineering computations through to quantum-enhanced optimisation and state management.

## EEI KPI (Energy-Efficiency Index Key Performance Indicator)

A performance metric defined under the → Prime Material Ontology that measures the energy conversion efficiency of hydrogen as prime material — from storage through fuel cell conversion to delivered electrical power. Replaces traditional fuel-burn metrics with a hydrogen-native efficiency measure.

## GAIA-SPACE-LAUNCHER

A quantum-hybrid three-stage launch system programme within the AEROSPACEMODEL ecosystem. Shares the OPT-IN 5-axis taxonomy, Git hooks infrastructure, and CI/CD suite with the AMPEL360 Q100 programme.

## GENESIS

The Knowledge Space within the → KISS architecture. GENESIS is the domain of conceptual models, ontological definitions, and design intent — the "what and why" of engineering knowledge, as distinct from the "what is recorded" of the Information Space (→ SSOT).

## GOV-CHARTER-SPLIT-001

The Governance Charter that formally separates the aircraftmodel.eu and aerospacemodel.com domains, establishing distinct configuration management authorities, publication scopes, and editorial governance for each domain while maintaining shared lifecycle infrastructure.

## HOB / NHOB (Hazardous On-Board / Non-Hazardous On-Board)

A binary safety ontology classifying all on-board systems, subsystems, and components as either safety-critical (HOB) or non-safety-critical (NHOB). The classification drives design assurance level allocation, redundancy requirements, and certification evidence depth across the AMPEL360 programme.

## HUELLΔ (Physical Asset Tracking System)

## IDEALE.eu / IDEALEeu Enterprise

## IEF (IDEALE Evidence Framework)

IEF evidence artifacts are portable, cryptographically anchored, and designed to satisfy multiple regulatory regimes (EASA, ESA, EU ESPR) from a single evidence base. → Teknia Tokens provide the economic incentive layer.

## IR (Introducción Retrospectiva)

An artifact type within the SPACET Q10 document scaffold (v7.6 taxonomy). IR is tied to LC Phase P000 and provides retrospective introductions — contextualising a topic by reviewing its historical evolution and prior decisions before presenting current state.

## KISS (Knowledge and Information Standard System)

The epistemological architecture separating Knowledge Space (→ GENESIS) from Information Space (→ SSOT). KISS asserts that design knowledge (conceptual models, intent, rationale) and design information (recorded data, configurations, evidence) are fundamentally different concerns requiring different management disciplines.

## KNOT (Known Unknown — Topological Space)

A formally identified uncertainty occupying a defined position in the programme's topological problem space. The "Topological Space" framing is deliberate: KNOTs are not isolated risks but nodes in a connected topology — they have adjacency, boundary, and continuity relationships with other KNOTs, enabling spillover impact measurement and cross-KNOT dependency analysis. Each KNOT is logged in `KNOTS.csv` within LC01_PROBLEM_STATEMENT, assigned an initial residual uncertainty score (0–100), and linked to expected → KNUs that will drive it toward closure. KNOTs are the demand signal for engineering work — no artifact should be produced without a parent KNOT declaring why it is needed.

### KNOT Closure Criteria

A KNOT is CLOSED when:
1. All planned KNUs reach COMPLETE or ACCEPTED status
2. Residual drops to or below target (e.g., 100 → ≤10)
3. All PUB artifacts pass BREX validation
4. All trace links resolve (no dangling references)
5. Signoffs captured in evidence pack
6. TT rewards distributed and logged

## KNU (Knowledge Unit)

A concrete engineering or publication artifact that addresses a → KNOT. KNUs are planned in `KNU_PLAN.csv`, typed (REQ / ICD / ANA / TEST / SAF / CM / PUB), and mapped to specific lifecycle phases and SSOT/CSDB locations. The cumulative effect of KNUs reduces the parent KNOT's residual uncertainty score.

## L-S-D / LSD(t) Framework

A unified geometric-physical ontology for the AMPEL360 Q100 formalising three coupled physical domains under a single time-dependent mathematical framework:

| Letter | Formal Domain | Mnemonic |
|--------|---------------|----------|
| L | Lift (aerodynamic) | Lifting |
| S | Storage/Source (electrochemical) | Sustaining |
| D | Distribution/Design (structural) | Dragging |

The mnemonic "Lifting — Sustaining — Dragging, all the time" captures the physical intuition: the airframe continuously lifts, the energy system continuously sustains, and the structure continuously carries (drags) loads — all simultaneously and interdependently, hence the time-dependent coupling LSD(t). Provides the theoretical basis for multi-physics coupled analysis across the aircraft.

## MTL (Method Token Ledger)

The central ledger infrastructure of the AEROSPACEMODEL tokenization economy. MTL is the authoritative record of all → Teknia Token (TT) transactions — reward distributions, fee deductions, spillover credits, and closure settlements — anchored by a SHA-256 hash chain (`finance/ledger.json`).

MTL serves three converging functions:

**1. Tokenization Economy Backbone.** Every KNOT closure, KNU acceptance, and cross-programme contribution is recorded as an MTL transaction. The ledger is the single source of truth for token balances, pool allocations, and distribution history. Without MTL, the TT incentive system has no verifiable state.

**2. Rapid, Direct, and Secure RAG (Retrieval-Augmented Generation).** MTL entries are structured for machine retrieval: each transaction carries the full provenance chain (KNOT_ID, KNU_ID, AoR, effort, impact, timestamp, hash). This makes the ledger a first-class RAG source — AI agents can query MTL directly to ground responses in verified engineering contribution history, token state, and artifact provenance without intermediate indexing layers. Retrieval is direct (no indirection through secondary databases), rapid (hash-indexed), and secure (chain-verified).

**3. Decryption and Access Control Gate.** MTL transactions carry cryptographic anchors that serve as access-control primitives. Artifact access, evidence-pack retrieval, and publication-scope delivery can be gated by MTL token state — ensuring that only stakeholders with verified contributions or authorised token holdings can decrypt and access controlled engineering data. The ledger thus bridges the incentive layer and the information-security layer into a single verifiable mechanism.

## NIB (Non-Inference Boundary)

The NIB taxonomy crosses with five safety impact levels to produce a 6×5 matrix governing where autonomous inference may operate and where human authority is mandated.

## PATH→MTL Pipeline

"Pre Approved Template Header → Method Token Ledger" — the end-to-end traceable workflow from content authoring to ledger settlement. The PATH header (typically embedded as YAML front matter) declares the template identity, approval status, authoring AoR, and target KNOT/KNU linkage. Upon artifact acceptance, the pipeline commits a transaction to the → MTL (Method Token Ledger), recording the contribution, computing the TT reward, and anchoring the cryptographic hash. This ensures every content generation action is template-governed at entry (PATH) and token-settled at exit (MTL).

## PR-O-RO PROTOROBOTICS

A certification framework for hybrid cyber-physical artifacts — systems combining physical hardware ("body") with embedded intelligence ("brain"). PR-O-RO defines certification evidence requirements, test protocols, and assurance levels needed to approve artifacts that span the traditional hardware/software boundary. Supported by the → Body+Brain Identity Register.

## Prime Material Ontology

A conceptual reframing treating hydrogen not merely as "fuel" but as the programme's "prime material" — the fundamental energy carrier, thermal management medium, and circularity substrate around which the entire aircraft system is designed. Introduces the → EEI KPI and reframes ATA 28 as → C² CELL.

## Profile Constructor (Three-Axis)

A configuration mechanism constructing the operational profile of a specific aircraft instance from three independent axes: mission type, environmental envelope, and fleet role. The profile constructor generates applicability codes that drive content filtering in the IETP.

## QAIM-2 (Quantum-Augmented Integrated Model, Version 2)

A hybrid optimisation architecture coupling classical engineering solvers with quantum computing resources for specific subproblems (combinatorial optimisation, quantum chemistry, materials simulation). "Augmented" emphasises that quantum capabilities enhance — rather than replace — the classical integrated model. QAIM-2 defines interface protocols, problem decomposition strategies, and result-validation gates for quantum-classical hybrid workflows.

## SKELETON (14-Folder Lifecycle Standard Repository Tentative Structure)

The canonical directory structure required at every ATA chapter node within the OPT-IN framework. The "tentative" designation acknowledges that the structure is a governed starting point — a standard scaffold that each programme instantiates and then populates, with the understanding that specific nodes may be tailored through controlled deviation. Consists of 14 lifecycle phase folders (LC01–LC14) plus a PUB subtree. CI validators enforce SKELETON completeness across all 79 chapters.

## SPACET Q10

A programme within the AEROSPACEMODEL ecosystem with its own document scaffold under the v7.6 taxonomy. Introduces artifact types → IR (Introducción Retrospectiva) and PM, each tied to specific lifecycle phases.

## SSOT (Single Source of Truth)

The authoritative engineering layer in the dual-layer architecture. SSOT contains all lifecycle engineering artifacts — requirements, safety evidence, designs, ICDs, V&V artifacts, configuration records — organised by lifecycle phase (LC01–LC14). SSOT is the system of record from which all publications are derived.

## SSOT + PUB (Dual-Layer Architecture)

The foundational architectural principle of the AEROSPACEMODEL publication system. Engineering truth (SSOT) is strictly separated from publishable deliverables (PUB). Publication content is always derived from governed engineering data and never authored in isolation. This pattern applies at every level of the OPT-IN hierarchy.

## TALF (Technical Artifact Lifecycle Format)

A format specification governing the lifecycle metadata, versioning, and traceability attributes accompanying every technical artifact. TALF integration ensures artifacts carry their full provenance chain from creation through all governance gates.

## Teknia Token (TT)

The incentive and accounting unit of the AEROSPACEMODEL tokenomics system. 1 TT = 360 degrees. Genesis supply: 2,000,000,000 TT. Rewards are allocated per KNOT and distributed upon closure using the effort-plus-impact formula:

```
w_i = α · Ê_i + (1 − α) · Î_i
T_i = P_k · w_i
```

| Parameter | Meaning | Default |
|-----------|---------|---------|
| P_k | Pool amount (TT) for the KNOT | per KNOT |
| α | Effort weight | 0.30 |
| Ê_i | Normalised effort: E_i / Σ E_i | — |
| Î_i | Normalised impact: I_i / Σ I_i | — |
| I_i | Effective impact: ΔR_k,i + λ·S_i | — |
| S_i | Spillover: Σ(a_k→j · ΔR_j,i) | — |
| λ | Spillover multiplier | 0.50 |

### TT Fee Structure (π-Tier)

| Tier | Rate | Application |
|------|------|-------------|
| π₁ | 0.314% | Standard transfers |
| π₂ | 0.99% | Elevated transfers |
| π₃ | 3.14% | Premium transfers |
| Reward | 0.50% | Reward distributions |

Ledger: All transactions are committed to the → MTL (Method Token Ledger), anchored by SHA-256 hash chain at `finance/ledger.json`. MTL is the authoritative state of the tokenization economy.

## TFA (Threading Functional Architecture / Top Figure Architecture)

The systems engineering architecture underpinning all AEROSPACEMODEL programme work. The dual name reflects dual function: as **Threading Functional Architecture**, TFA defines 15 engineering domains connected by quantum-classical bridge layers (→ CB→QB→UE→FE→FWD→QS), ensuring all functional threads are traceable across domain boundaries; as **Top Figure Architecture**, TFA serves as the programme's highest-level architectural diagram — the single figure from which all system decomposition descends.

## TLI v2.1 (ToC Level Index / Top Level Instructions)

The version 2.1 registry serving a dual role: as **ToC Level Index**, it provides the canonical table-of-contents structure for each ATA chapter, section, or subject level, defining the hierarchical organisation of content; as **Top Level Instructions**, it carries the authoritative lifecycle phase definitions (LC00–LC14), their entry/exit criteria, mandatory artifacts, and governance gates at the ATA, section, or subject level. TLI operates at whichever granularity is appropriate — full ATA chapter, section (xx-yy), or individual subject (xx-yy-zz).

## TOP-LAP (Multi-Dimensional Logical State Process)

A process framework modelled as a multi-dimensional logical state machine with quantum readiness gates. TOP-LAP formalises the state transitions of engineering artifacts across lifecycle phases, with each gate requiring specific evidence conditions before transition is permitted.

## BWB (Blended Wing Body)

The primary airframe configuration for the AMPEL360 Q100. In a BWB, wing and fuselage merge into a continuous lifting-body shape, maximising internal volume and aerodynamic efficiency while reducing wetted area. Within the AEROSPACEMODEL ecosystem, the BWB configuration is designated as the **future reference for flying legacies** — the architecture against which all successor airframe concepts will be measured and from which design heritage will be inherited.

## WTW (Wing-Tube-Wing)

The alternative aircraft configuration path (alongside BWB) within the AMPEL360 programme. WTW represents a more conventional tube-and-wing layout adapted for hydrogen propulsion. The → GOV-CHARTER-SPLIT-001 separates BWB and WTW configuration management domains.
