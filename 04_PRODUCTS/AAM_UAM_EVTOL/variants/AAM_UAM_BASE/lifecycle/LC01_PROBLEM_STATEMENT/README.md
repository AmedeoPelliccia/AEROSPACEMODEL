## Problem Statement

The Advanced Air Mobility / Urban Air Mobility market requires a safe, certifiable, and economically viable eVTOL aircraft that operates as part of a **network system**. The mission is defined by the network, not the vehicle alone — a single eVTOL has no utility without vertiport pairs, airspace corridors, charging infrastructure, and UTM integration. The "system" being certified is the entire operation.

## Operational Concept

The AAM/UAM eVTOL performs missions between vertiport pairs in urban and metropolitan areas, operating in U-space corridors at 150–300 m AGL. The mission profile consists of:

1. **Vertical climb** — from Vertiport A ground pad to ~30 m, then to transition altitude (~150 m)
2. **Transition** — conversion from hover to forward flight mode at ~150–200 m AGL
3. **Cruise** — sustained forward flight at 150–250 km/h TAS, 150–300 m AGL (U-space corridor)
4. **Deceleration** — reduction from cruise speed approaching Vertiport B
5. **Vertical descent** — from transition altitude (~150 m) to ground pad at Vertiport B

## Key Drivers

- Network utility: vertiport infrastructure, UTM integration, charging logistics
- Certification under EASA SC-VTOL (2026–2027 timeline)
- Electric propulsion: battery-dominant, 4–18 rotors, optional tilt-rotor
- Noise constraint: ≤80 dB(A) at vertiport boundary (community acceptance)
- Gate-to-gate time competitive with ground transport: 12–35 min
