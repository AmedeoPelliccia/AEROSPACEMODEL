## Problem Context

Persistent stratospheric coverage occupies a capability gap between conventional
aircraft (limited to hours-to-days endurance at ≤14 km) and LEO satellites
(limited by orbital mechanics to periodic overpasses with revisit gaps). A High
Altitude Pseudo-Satellite (HAPS) operating at 18–22 km can provide continuous,
localised coverage with near-satellite perspective and near-aircraft latency.

The HAPS-SE variant uses solar-electric propulsion to achieve multi-month
autonomous station-keeping without fuel logistics, addressing missions in earth
observation, telecommunications relay, atmospheric science, and
surveillance/reconnaissance.

## Assessment Objective

Analyse the operational gap between satellite and conventional aircraft coverage
and determine whether a solar-electric HAPS platform can fill that gap with a
viable, certifiable, and economically sustainable system.
